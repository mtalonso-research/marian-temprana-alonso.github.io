Marian Temprana Alonso

Bio

Hi, I’m Marian Temprana Alonso, a Ph.D. candidate at the solid Lab (Security, Optimization, and Learning for InterDependent networks) in the Knight Foundation School of Computing and Information Sciences at Florida International University. My research focuses on information theory, signal processing, and machine learning, especially for energy-efficient compression, communication, and inference. I got my B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics from FIU in 2022, and since then I’ve worked on a range of projects involving task-based quantization, nonlinear analog processing in wireless systems, sensor fusion, and deep learning for multi-modal CSI compression. Some of those works has been published in IEEE conferences like ISIT and ITW, as well as jourals like TVT. I am a recipient of the GAANN fellowship, as well as a Grace Hopper Celebration Scholar and honorary member of the IEEEE IT Society S&O subcomittee. I also spent time as a year-round research intern at Sandia National Labs working on data analysis for power grid resillience.
