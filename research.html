Research Projects

  • Resilient Power Grid Analytics for Large Contingencies Collaboration with Sandia National Laboratories and Los Alamos National Laboratory (2023–2025). Applied data mining and advanced analytics to post-process high-contingency power grid simulations. Identified patterns in cascading events towards resilience planning. Developed a regression-based predictive framework to assess topology changes and mitigate the impact of High-Altitude Electromagnetic Pulses (HEMPs) on the grid:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.
  • Temporal Knowledge Graphs for Reasoning and Prediction in Traffic Scenes Collaboration with National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University and IIT Kanpur (2022–2025). Built a pipeline combining YOLO-based object detection and MiDas depth perception, overlaying depth heatmaps on traffic frames. Leveraged LLMs to generate propositional logic statements and encode temporal knowledge graphs. Integrated these into a Graph Neural Network (GNN) for prediction and analysis in autonomous driving:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.
  • Reinforcement Learning-Based Optimization for Quantization Designed an RL framework to approximate optimal quantization configurations. Extended the CORTICAL algorithm for mutual information approximation and maximization in discrete settings. Validated the framework with simulations in wireless communications and task-based quantization:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
  • Deep Learning-Driven Multi-Modal Sensor Fusion for CSI Compression Collaboration with NYCU, IIT Kanpur, and Arizona State University. Proposed a system model for CSI estimation using multi-modal side information at the base station. Evaluated estimation accuracy under different modalities and compression rates using deep neural networks:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  • Task-Based Quantization with Nonlinear Analog Processing Collaboration with the Weizmann Institute of Science and University of Melbourne. Investigated task-based quantization where the task differs from the observed measurements. Demonstrated that nonlinear analog processing reduces distortion and improves rate-distortion performance:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  • Energy-Efficient Wireless Communication Using Low-Resolution ADCs Collaboration with University of California, Irvine. Analyzed MIMO communication rates under low-resolution ADCs for energy efficiency. Justified incorporating nonlinear operators via theoretical and numerical analysis, showing rate and efficiency gains:contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.
  • Consensus-Based Sensor Fusion Studied tradeoffs between accuracy and consensus in distributed estimation. Decomposed the fusion function into orthonormal basis components in Hilbert space. Formulated optimization problems for optimal fusion algorithms:contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
