Research Projects

  • Temporal Knowledge Graphs for Reasoning and Prediction in Traffic Scenes [C1]
    • Collaboration with National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University and IIT Kanpur.
    • Built a pipeline combining YOLO-based object detection and MiDas depth perception, overlaying depth heatmaps on traffic frames.
    • Leveraged LLMs to generate propositional logic statements and encode temporal knowledge graphs.
    • Integrated these into a Graph Neural Network (GNN) for prediction and analysis in autonomous driving.
  • Reinforcement Learning-Based Optimization for Quantization [J1]
    • Designed an RL framework to approximate optimal quantization configurations.
    • Extended the CORTICAL algorithm for mutual information approximation and maximization in discrete settings.
    • Validated the framework with simulations in wireless communications and task-based quantization.
  • Deep Learning-Driven Multi-Modal Sensor Fusion for CSI Compression [C2]
    • Collaboration with NYCU, IIT Kanpur, and Arizona State University.
    • Proposed a system model for CSI estimation using multi-modal side information at the base station.
    • Evaluated estimation accuracy under different modalities and compression rates using deep neural networks.
  • Task-Based Quantization with Nonlinear Analog Processing [J2]
    • Collaboration with the Weizmann Institute of Science and University of Melbourne.
    • Investigated task-based quantization where the task differs from the observed measurements.
    • Demonstrated that nonlinear analog processing reduces distortion and improves rate-distortion performance.
  • Energy-Efficient Wireless Communication Using Low-Resolution ADCs [Git]
    • Collaboration with University of California, Irvine.
    • Analyzed MIMO communication rates under low-resolution ADCs for energy efficiency.
    • Justified incorporating nonlinear operators via theoretical and numerical analysis, showing rate and efficiency gains.
  • Consensus-Based Sensor Fusion [C3]
    • Studied tradeoffs between accuracy and consensus in distributed estimation.
    • Decomposed the fusion function into orthonormal basis components in Hilbert space.
    • Formulated optimization problems for optimal fusion algorithms.
